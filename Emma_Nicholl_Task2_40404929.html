Why Merlin is the greatest TV show of all time

BBC's Merlin (2008-2012) might at first seem to be one of many mediocre fantasy shows produced post-20001, best put by its Wikipedia page as

A British fantasy-adventure drama television programme, loosely based on the Arthurian legends regarding the close relations of Merlin and King Arthur. The programme is a reimagining of the legend, in which young warlock Merlin is sent to the kingdom of Camelot by his mother because of his "special gift". After saving Prince Arthur's life in the first episode, he becomes Arthur's manservant. Merlin soon learns that the reason for his gift is to protect Arthur

However, as an occasional watcher of the genre I feel best placed to judge them all, and have (reasonably) concluded that Merlin best encompasses everything absolutely insane about this era of television - from its ridiculously confusing plot to the wildly variable accents of what is apparently the same region

Thus, I have created this website (and this handy navigation tool) to demonstrate my point, but to also spread the joy.

My personal opinions on why Merlin is the GOAT

As this website has demonstrated, I am deeply, deeply invested in promoting this show to as many people as possible. Therefore, I have decided to summarise all of my feelings into this brief list of pros and cons of watching the show (but even the cons are pros if you don't care).

Pros

  • Short and sweet (65 episodes in total)
  • Requires absolutely no brain power to watch
  • Very very nice costumes
  • Really great guest stars
  • The soundtrack is beautiful
  • You'll never quite be sure what happens next
  • Merlin has a hugely active online fandom post-2012, and has a wealth of fan-created activity to enjoy after you create the show

Cons (which are still pros if you aren't picky)

  • The CGI is horrendous
  • The plot is nonsensical in multiple aspects
  • The geography of the show makes no sense
  • The historical time period of the show is all over the place - the costumes are medieval yet the legends are from the 4th/5th century
  • The finale is absolutely wild in the worst way
  • The fanfiction is something else (allegedly)
